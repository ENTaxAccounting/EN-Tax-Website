Depreciation Strategy Tips
Timing Considerations

• Assets placed in service late in the year may have reduced first-year depreciation
• Consider Section 179 expensing for immediate deductions
• Bonus depreciation may apply to certain assets

Asset Classification

• Computers and software are typically 5-year property
• Office furniture is usually 7-year property
• Real estate has much longer recovery periods

Tax Planning

• Higher depreciation in early years can defer taxes
• Consider your expected future tax rates
• Maintain detailed records for IRS compliance

Business Asset Depreciation Essentials

MACRS Depreciation System

The Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System (MACRS) is the primary depreciation method for business assets:

  • 3-year property: Software, research equipment, tractor units
  • 5-year property: Computers, office equipment, automobiles
  • 7-year property: Office furniture, machinery, equipment
  • 10-year property: Equipment, single-purpose structures
  • 15-year property: Improvements, landscaping, roads
  • 20-year property: Farm buildings, certain equipment

Section 179 & Bonus Depreciation

Accelerated depreciation options can provide immediate tax benefits:

  • Section 179: Up to $1,160,000 immediate expensing (2024)
  • Bonus depreciation: 80% immediate deduction (2024)
  • Applies to new and used qualified property
  • Business income limitations may apply
  • Phase-out thresholds for large purchases
  • Consider timing for maximum tax benefit

Depreciation Record Keeping

Maintain proper documentation to support your depreciation deductions:

  • Purchase receipts and invoices
  • Date placed in service documentation
  • Asset cost basis calculations
  • Business use percentage records
  • Depreciation schedule tracking
  • Disposition and sale records
