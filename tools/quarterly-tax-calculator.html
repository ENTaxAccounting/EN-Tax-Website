Important Notes

• Payments are due on the 15th of the month following each quarter

• If the 15th falls on a weekend or holiday, the due date moves to the next business day

• You can make payments online at irs.gov/payments or by phone

• Consider setting calendar reminders 1-2 weeks before each due date

Understanding Quarterly Tax Payments

When Quarterly Payments Are Required

Self-employed individuals and businesses must make quarterly payments when they expect to owe $1,000 or more in taxes and don't have sufficient withholding:

  • Independent contractors and freelancers
  • Small business owners and sole proprietors
  • Real estate investors with rental income
  • Anyone with significant investment income
  • Those with multiple income sources

Safe Harbor Protection

Avoid underpayment penalties by meeting safe harbor requirements based on your prior year tax liability:

  • Pay 100% of last year's tax (if AGI ≤ $150,000)
  • Pay 110% of last year's tax (if AGI > $150,000)
  • Pay 90% of current year's tax liability
  • Make all payments by quarterly due dates
  • Use Form 1040ES for accurate calculations
