Business Entity Comparison

Compare key features of different business entity types to understand their advantages and disadvantages.

📄 Sole Proprietorship

Best For: Single-owner businesses, freelancers, consultants

Liability: 🔴 No protection - personal assets at risk

Taxes: 🟡 Pass-through taxation on personal return

Complexity: 🟢 Very simple setup and maintenance

Cost: 🟢 Minimal costs

Pros:
  • Simple and inexpensive
  • Complete control
  • No separate tax filings
  • Easy to dissolve
Cons:
  • Unlimited personal liability
  • Harder to raise capital
  • Self-employment taxes
  • Limited business life
🏢 LLC (Limited Liability Company)

Best For: Small to medium businesses, real estate, multiple owners

Liability: 🟢 Personal asset protection

Taxes: 🟡 Flexible tax elections (pass-through or corporate)

Complexity: 🟡 Moderate setup, flexible management

Cost: 🟡 Moderate filing fees and maintenance

Pros:
  • Limited liability protection
  • Tax flexibility
  • Management flexibility
  • Credibility with customers
Cons:
  • More paperwork than sole proprietorship
  • Self-employment taxes (if elected)
  • State-specific rules vary
  • Potential franchise taxes
🏦 S Corporation

Best For: Small businesses with active owners wanting payroll tax savings

Liability: 🟢 Personal asset protection

Taxes: 🟢 Pass-through, potential SE tax savings

Complexity: 🟠 Moderate - payroll requirements

Cost: 🟡 Moderate setup, payroll costs

Pros:
  • Limited liability protection
  • Self-employment tax savings
  • Pass-through taxation
  • Business credibility
Cons:
  • Payroll requirements
  • Ownership restrictions
  • More compliance requirements
  • Reasonable salary requirements
🏢 C Corporation

Best For: Large businesses, raising capital, going public

Liability: 🟢 Strong personal asset protection

Taxes: 🔴 Double taxation (corporate + personal)

Complexity: 🔴 High - board meetings, bylaws, etc.

Cost: 🔴 Higher setup and maintenance costs

Pros:
  • Strongest liability protection
  • Easier to raise capital
  • Unlimited ownership
  • Perpetual existence
Cons:
  • Double taxation
  • Complex compliance
  • Expensive to maintain
  • Board requirements

Key Entity Selection Factors

🛡️ Liability Protection

Different entities provide varying levels of personal asset protection. LLCs and corporations offer strong protection, while sole proprietorships provide none. Consider professional liability insurance regardless of entity type.

📊 Tax Implications

Entity choice significantly affects your tax burden including self-employment taxes, pass-through vs double taxation, deduction opportunities, and state-specific considerations.

📈 Growth & Investment

Your growth plans should influence entity selection. C Corps are best for raising capital, while LLCs offer flexibility for multiple owners and simpler structures.

💼 Operational Complexity

Each entity type comes with different administrative requirements, compliance obligations, and maintenance costs. Balance complexity with benefits for your situation.
